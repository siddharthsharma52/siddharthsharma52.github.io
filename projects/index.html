Summer Intern at Viterbi School of Engineering, University of Southern California, USA
Summer 2013

I worked in the BioRC research project group led by Dr. Alice C. Parker in the Ming Hsieh School of Electrical Engineering. The target of the research group is to mimic the human brain’s neural structure through analog VLSI and nanotechnology. Within the BioRC group, I was involved with the C.Elegans project team with target to mimic the neural structure of C.Elegans worm. I worked in a team of three, with a EE masters student as the team head and the other member was another intern selected in the Viterbi Summer Undergraduate Research Experience (SURE).

When I joined Dr. Parker’s research team everyone was building neurons manually which was very tedious and time consuming and also not very efficient especially because we are not sure of the design and structure and we constantly keep making changes. Following an idea of our team leader to automate the process of building the neuron structures using scripts, our team came up with the plan of using python and Shell scripts to create the structure. Later we integrated that with Ocean Scripting. We created and presented the prototype software functioning for one neuron in the research group meeting and a poster for the same was presented by my team-mate Chase Cook in the Poster Presentation organized for all SURE interns.

Projects Undertaken at CEDT, NSIT:


  • ”Pac–Man” on Digital Storage Oscilloscope:

    Working in a team of three, I developed a single player "Pac-Man" game on a Digital Storage Oscillloscope (DSO) as part of Atmel Oscilloscope Design Challenge. The graphic display was established in X-Y mode of DSO using two R-2R ladder-network circuit based Digital to Analog Converter and the gameplay through a basic controller interfaced with Arduino
    Project Video


  • Non-Invasive Heart-Rate Monitor:

    Developed a heart-rate monitor using an IR sensor by employing the principles of Plethysmography. The signal was plotted as a graph using Processing. Developed the circuit on a general-purpose glass epoxy PCB after breadboard prototyping


  • "Snake" Game on an 8x8 LED Matrix:

    Developed the game "Snake", played through a basic controller interfaced with Arduino
    Project Video


  • PCB Design and Fabrication:

    Designed the PCB for a 5VDC voltage-regulated power supply with maximum current drive of 1A. I designed the schematic on EagleCAD. The power supply provided fold-back current limiting for short-circuit protection. Fabricated the PCB using acid-etching method
