Summer Research Intern at Viterbi School of Engineering, University of Southern California, USA
Summer 2013

During this internship, I worked in the BioRC research project group led by Dr. Alice C. Parker in the Ming Hsieh Department of Electrical Engineering. The target of the research group is to mimic the human brain’s neural structure through analog VLSI and nanotechnology. Within the BioRC group, I was involved with the C.Elegans project team with target to mimic the neural structure of C.Elegans worm.

I worked with Cadence Virtuoso Spectre – the circuit simulation software, and automated the process of neuromorphic circuit design at the research group, bypassing the GUI through python, and Cadence OCEAN scripts. The software prototype we created can be used to connect various analog neuromorphic circuit units (Axon Hillock, Synapse, etc) that had been developed at the research group, according to user's circuit connection information through a text file. When complete, this software would create the 302-neuron circuit of the C.Elegans worm with greater ease, as compared with the effort and time taken for the circuit created through Cadence GUI.

We had developed the software prototype for automatic creation of one neuron. The future work of this project is to enable automation of connecting various neurons. For this, machine learning (genetic algorithm) could be applied to calculate "weights" of the various interconnections between neurons. This could be tested using the touch sensitivity neuromorphic circuit of a C.Elegans worm.

Research Intern at mLabs Research
September 2012 - May 2013

Here, I was involved in designing various Arduino and Raspberry Pi based circuits, integrating devices like tft-touch display, 802.11-module, DACs and analog switch matrix microchips by employing bus communication protocols such as SPI and I2C.

I got introduced to memristors and their implementation in the field of neuromorphic circuits and was involved in developing modules for memristor characterization using Arduino.

I also worked in the hardware development team of the prototype for mLabs' first ever commercial product, Bit-by-Bit (B3) – a product that enables everyone to design their own Internet of Things enabled hardware. B3 provides a completely automated data acquisition and analysis system, along with an arbitrary waveform generator, using a RaspberryPi-based board. The concept is patented, and is supported by Microsoft Ventures in London.
